Best AWD Vehicles for Teton Pass — What Actually Works in Winter

By Yancy Garcia • Stone's Auto Group • Jackson, WY • March 29, 2026

Teton Pass separates Jackson from Victor. It's 8,431 feet at the summit, steep on both sides, and in winter it's one of the most demanding commuter roads in the country. If you drive it daily, your vehicle choice matters more than almost anywhere else. I've spent years helping people find the best AWD vehicles for Teton Pass, and here's what I've learned.

I'm Yancy Garcia at Stone's Auto Group in Jackson, WY. I've seen what makes it over the pass and what ends up sideways in the ditch. This is my honest list.

What Teton Pass Demands From a Vehicle

Before I name specific models, let's talk about what the pass requires:

  • Ground clearance — The road gets rutted, icy, and the plows leave berms. You need at least 8 inches.
  • Real AWD or 4WD — Front-wheel-drive with "AWD assist" won't cut it. You need a system that sends real torque to all four wheels.
  • Good tires — This matters more than the vehicle itself. Studded snow tires or quality all-terrains are non-negotiable.
  • Reliable brakes — The descent is steep. You need brakes that handle repeated heavy braking without fading.
  • Engine braking — Downhill assist or a manual mode that lets you hold lower gears saves your brakes and your nerves.

Top Picks for the Best AWD Vehicles for Teton Pass

Subaru Outback / Forester — The default Teton Pass vehicle, and for good reason. Symmetrical AWD is always on, ground clearance is solid, and the boxer engine sits low for a better center of gravity. I see more Outbacks on the pass than anything else. If you're commuting daily and want reliability plus fuel economy, this is the safe choice.

Toyota 4Runner — For people who want body-on-frame confidence. The 4Runner's part-time 4WD with a locking rear diff handles the worst the pass throws at you. It's overbuilt in the best way. The trade-off is fuel economy — you'll feel it at the pump.

Jeep Grand Cherokee — The Quadra-Drive II system in the Overland and Summit trims is genuinely impressive. The air suspension adjusts ride height for deep snow. It's more comfortable than the 4Runner on long drives. Watch for electrical gremlins on high-mileage units.

Toyota Tacoma — Half the ski patrol in Jackson drives a Tacoma. Compact enough to handle the tight switchbacks, capable enough to get through anything. The manual transmission in the older models gives you total control on the descent.

Chevrolet Tahoe / Suburban — If you need to move a family plus gear, the full-size GM SUVs with the 4WD transfer case handle the pass well. Heavy means stable. Just make sure you have good tires and use the tow/haul mode for engine braking.

Vehicles I'd Avoid on Teton Pass

Low-clearance sedans, even with AWD. Front-wheel-drive anything. Vehicles with CVT transmissions that overheat on long grades — some Nissan models struggle here. Anything on all-season tires that are more than two seasons old.

The best AWD vehicles for Teton Pass are the ones that match the conditions to your actual life. Commuting solo? Outback. Family of five with gear? Tahoe. Weekend warrior? 4Runner. There's no single right answer.
